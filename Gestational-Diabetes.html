What is Gestational Diabetes?

Gestational Diabetes is a condition in which a woman without diabetes will develop high blood sugar levels during a pregnancy.

Symptoms

  • Mother:
    • Yeast infections
    • Frequent urination
    • Extreme thirst and hunger or fatigue
    • Higher risk of Precclapsia during a pregnancy
    • Blurred vision
  • Baby:
    • Higher birth weight
    • Premature birth
    • Low blood sugar
    • Obesity and type 2 diabetes risk

However, most women do not have noticable symptoms

Risk Factors

You are at risk if you:

  • Are Overweight/Obese
  • Are known to have had gestational diabetes before
  • Lack physical activity
  • Have family history of type 2 diabetes
  • Have had a baby that weighed more than 9 pounds
  • Have had Polycystic Ovary Syndrome
  • Are African American, Native American, Asian American,
    Pacific Islander, or of Hispanic descent
  • Are above 35
  • Have a history of recurrent miscarriages

If you have Gestational Diabetes, make frequent visits to your doctor,
where they will monitor your blood sugar levels and your babiy's health. Here are some tips to avoid complications.

  1. Stay active and exercise through pregnancy
  2. Eat smaller and more frequent meals
      Diet Plan
    • Lean meats
    • Healthy fats
    • Whole grains
    • Low-fat dairy products
    • Non-starchy vegetables
    • Fruits
  3. Keep check of your glucose levels
  4. Avoid sugary foods and drinks
  5. Update your doctor and have frequent check-ups
